Straight answers

The things you would ask if we were already talking

I have tried this before and it did not work

An owner told me he spent twelve thousand dollars over three months with an SEO company. What he got was tire kickers. He was not scammed, though. He bought a real thing. It just solved a problem he did not have.

Lead companies

Angi, Thumbtack, whatever the version is in your line of work. You pay less than a lead is really worth, so they sell that same one to four or five others. By the time you call, the person has talked to three people, is price shopping, and is irritated the phone rang again. Two kinds of business win there: the ones with somebody paid to sit and dial the second a lead lands, and the ones who keep calling long after everyone else quit. If neither is you, you are funding somebody else's operation.

SEO

It brings the best kind of person there is, somebody who needs it right now and is looking for you specifically. The catch is the clock. You pay every month for something you might see in six, and a report does not cover payroll.

Ads and agencies

They can work. But if there is nothing on the other end to catch the people they send, you are paying to point them somewhere they bounce off. The ad platform gets paid either way, so it is built to send volume at the lowest price, not the right person.

None of those are bad businesses. They are answers to a question you were not asking.

Why did you build it before asking me for anything?

Normally somebody calls, pitches you, you pay, and then you find out what you bought. We built yours first. Not because we are generous, but because of how that conversation goes otherwise.

If we describe a website to you, you have to take our word for it, and you have probably taken somebody's word for it before and it cost you. If you are looking at the actual thing, there is nothing to take our word for. You can tell us it is wrong, that we got your services wrong, or that the colours are ugly.

How long before I see anything?

Reviews move first, usually inside the first few weeks. The people we send start arriving right away, which is the whole reason we do it. The slow part is coming up on your own when somebody searches, and that builds over months. Anyone shortening that for you is selling you something.

Inside 45 days you should be holding something real you can point at. More reviews than you had, and calls you can trace back to it. If nothing has moved by then you should be asking us hard questions. We would rather that than have you wait politely for six months.

Quick answers

Is there a contract or a setup fee?

Neither. There is nothing recurring, so there is nothing to get out of, and the number you pay is the whole cost.

I already have a website. What happens to it?

We can work from what you have or replace it, whichever makes more sense once we look. If your domain sits somewhere like GoDaddy or Squarespace it stays yours either way, and we handle the moving parts.

Where does a lead actually go?

Your phone. It arrives as a text with their name, their number, and what they said they need, the same as if they had texted you directly. If you are on a job or it is late, they still get a real answer back and it keeps talking to them until they are ready for you. Nothing sits in a dashboard waiting for you to notice it.

I am not technical. How much of this lands on me?

None of it. Nothing to log into, no password to keep somewhere, nothing to learn. When you want something changed, a service, a price, a photo, you tell us and it gets changed.

Do I have to change how I already work?

No. If you want calls coming to your cell the way they do now, they come to your cell. If you use a CRM, we work with it.

Business is good right now. Why would I do this?

Because busy is the only time you can. Nothing here shows up tomorrow. Reviews take weeks and coming up in a search takes months, so whatever you would want working for you in a slow month has to be started in a good one. The owners who regret it are the ones who go looking during the quiet stretch, when the only things built for that moment are the lead companies and the three month contracts.

What if I do not get five prospects a month?

Ask for your money back. It is an average across the first year, not a hard monthly floor, so one slow month does not decide it. You keep the website and the reviews either way.
