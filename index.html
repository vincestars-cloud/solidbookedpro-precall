What makes a site actually get you called

You could have your nephew build one, or use a builder that spits one out in ten minutes. You would end up with a website. Here is what is usually different about one built to make the phone ring. None of it is about how it looks.

01

A few questions instead of a blank box

Most sites have a Contact Us form. Name, email, and an empty box marked Message. That box asks a stranger to sit down and compose something. Most will not. They close the tab and you never knew they were there.

02

A page for each thing you do

If somebody looks for one specific service, they should land on a page about that service. Not a list they have to hunt through. People do not dig. If the first thing on the screen is not what they typed, they hit back.

03

A call button that follows them down the page

Most people find you on a phone, one hand, in the middle of doing something else. If they have to scroll back to the top to find your number, some of them will not.

If you have tried something like this before and it did not work

An owner told me he spent twelve thousand dollars over three months with an SEO company. What he got was tire kickers. He was not scammed, though. That is the part people get wrong. He bought a real thing. It just solved a problem he did not have.

Lead companies

Angi, Thumbtack, whatever the version is in your line of work. You pay less than a lead is really worth, so they sell that same one to four or five others to make their side work. By the time you call, the person has already talked to three people, is price shopping, and is irritated the phone rang again.

Two kinds of businesses win there. The ones with somebody paid to sit and dial the second a lead lands, and the ones who keep calling long after everyone else quit. If neither is you, you are funding somebody else's operation.

SEO

It brings the best kind of person there is. Somebody who needs it right now and is looking for you specifically. The catch is the clock. You pay every month for something you might see in six. And a report does not cover payroll.

Ads and agencies

They can work. But if there is nothing on the other end to catch the people they send, you are paying to point them somewhere they bounce off. And the ad platform gets paid either way, so it is built to send volume at the lowest price, not the right person.

None of those are bad businesses. They are answers to a question you were not asking.
